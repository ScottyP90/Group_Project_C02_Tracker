Countries Overall Co2

In 2018 CO2 emissions were estimated to be around 37.1bn tonnes worldwide.

In 2018 India’s CO2 emissions has seen a 6.3%. China has the second largest increases in CO2 emissions at 4.7%, the US was third at an increase of 2.5%.

Here is a list of the worst countries with the highest MtCO2 (metric tons of carbon dioxide) rate

  1. China:- 9839 MtCO2
  2. USA:- 5279 MtCO2
  3. India:- 2467 MtCO2
  4. Russia:- 1693 MtCO2
  5. Japan:- 1205 MtCO2

Deforestation

Deforestation is the conversion of forested areas to non-forest land for use such as arable land, pasture, urban use, logged area, or wasteland.

Forests cover 30% of the earth’s land.

Loss of forests contributes between 12% and 17% percent of annual global greenhouse gas emissions.

According to Rainforest Action Network, the United States has less than 5% of the world’s population yet consumes more than 30% of the world’s paper.

20% of the world’s oxygen is produced in the Amazon forest.

Recycling

Americans use more than 67 million tons of paper per year, or about 580 pounds per person.

Each ton of recycled paper can save 17 trees, 380 gallons of oil, three cubic yards of landfill space, 4,000 kilowatts of energy and 7,000 gallons of water!

Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled endlessly without loss in quality or purity.

One ton of carbon dioxide is reduced for every six tons of recycled container glass used in the manufacturing process.

Below is a list from 2017 of the top 5 countries for recycling and their recycling rate:

  1. Germany - 56.1%
  2. Austria - 53.8%
  3. South Korea - 53.7%
  4. Wales - 52.2%
  5. Switzerland & Italy- 49.7%
